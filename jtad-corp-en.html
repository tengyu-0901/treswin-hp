§ 01 — Mission
Every working person deserves
the right to choose
their own career path.

Japan's labor market has long operated on a binary: those who push people to change jobs, and those who tell them to endure. Neither side was there to protect the individual's sense of agency. J-TAD was founded to fill that void.

Mission
Why we exist, right now

To be the companion that enables every individual — alongside AI — to articulate their own career in their own words, and choose their path with conviction.

Vision
The society we build by 2035

A Japan where no matter where you live or what you earn, there's always someone — or something — to think through your career with you.

Position
Who we exist for

Workers in rural areas, low-income brackets, and small businesses — those who've been left outside the reach of traditional career support.

§ 03 Principles

Five principles we act by

Code Principle What it means
V-01 Stay neutral, always We don't take sides — not with "change jobs" and not with "stay put." The individual's sense of conviction is the only thing we optimize for. Eliminating conflicts of interest is central to our reason for existing.
V-02 Turn dissatisfaction into an asset Frustration and anger aren't problems — they're entry points to self-understanding. Emotion is data, and data is a compass. We never dismiss what someone is feeling about their work.
V-03 AI is a tool. Companionship is human. Technology exists to lower barriers to access. But the act of truly accompanying someone through something difficult must remain deeply human. We don't confuse what AI does with what people need.
V-04 Make growth visible We don't settle for "I guess I feel a bit better." We prove individual change with scores and certifications that hold up in the real world. Making progress invisible is a failure of support.
V-05 Reach where no one else has Rural Japan. Low-income workers. People at small companies. The gaps in support are exactly where we go. Any infrastructure that leaves people out is, by definition, unfinished.
